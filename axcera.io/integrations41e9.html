Questions? We have Answers

For additional questions, contact our team at hello@nexus.io
What types of systems can Nexus integrate with?

Nexus integrates with a wide range of systems commonly used by prop firms and brokers, including trading platforms, payment and payout providers, compliance and verification tools, analytics solutions, communication systems, and operational software. The integration framework is designed to support both core infrastructure and specialist third-party services.

Can we request a new integration that is not currently listed?

Yes. Clients can request new integrations at any time. Nexus continuously expands its integration ecosystem based on client demand, operational value, and long-term relevance, even if a tool is not currently listed on the integrations page.

How long does it typically take to enable a new integration?

Timelines depend on the complexity of the system, API maturity, and testing requirements. Some integrations can be enabled quickly, while others require more detailed validation. Nexus aligns expectations early and communicates clear milestones throughout the process.

How does Nexus prioritize new integration requests?

Integration requests are prioritized based on client demand, impact on operations, security and compliance considerations, and overall value to the ecosystem. Integrations that benefit multiple clients or improve core workflows are typically prioritized.

Does Nexus integrate partner solutions directly into its platform?

Yes. Nexus handles the technical integration directly where applicable, embedding partner solutions into its platform. This ensures a consistent and reliable experience for clients without requiring partners to build or maintain integrations themselves.

What is the typical process for becoming an integration partner?

The process begins by submitting a request through the Partners page. Nexus then reviews the solution’s relevance, technical feasibility, and alignment with client needs before defining next steps, scope, and potential integration timelines.
